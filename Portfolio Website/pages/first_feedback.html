Reflecting on My First Feedback

Lessons Learned

September 12, 2025 Personal Growth

Receiving My First Feedback

On September 12, 2025, I received my first structured feedback on my project work. This was an important moment in my learning journey, as it gave me clear insights into areas I needed to improve and areas where I was doing well.

Key Points of Feedback

  • Fix Navbar: The navigation bar needed adjustments to improve usability and consistency.
  • Upgrade Dashboard: Some elements of the dashboard required restructuring for chronological order and better user experience.
  • Blog Styling: The blog layout and styling needed refinement to look more polished and professional.
  • Improve Frequently Asked Questions: The FAQ page needed improvements in structure and readability.
  • Well-structured folders: My project organization was praised, showing that I had a clear and logical folder structure.
  • Good CSS: My CSS organization and use of styling were recognized as strong points.
  • Proper Indentation: Clean and consistent indentation made my code readable and maintainable.

Lessons Learned

This feedback session highlighted the importance of details and refinement. While the overall structure of my project was solid, small adjustments in design and layout made a big difference in user experience. It also showed me that even when the foundations are strong, there is always room for improvement.

Personal Growth

Receiving this feedback boosted my confidence and gave me a clear direction for improvement. I learned that constructive criticism is not about failure, but about identifying opportunities to grow. It reinforced the value of:

  • Paying attention to UI/UX details
  • Continuously refining code quality
  • Appreciating positive reinforcement for the things I did well

With this feedback, I feel more motivated to continue improving my work. Each round of feedback is a stepping stone toward becoming a better developer.
